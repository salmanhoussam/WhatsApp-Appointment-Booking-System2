The Journey

"A small olive hung on a silver-green branch, dreaming of becoming something more."
"One autumn morning, a gentle hand picked her and placed her in a wicker basket."
"She traveled to the old stone press, where the air smelled of earth and ancestors. There, she and her sisters were poured into a large stone basin.

A heavy millstone, turned by strong arms, began to roll over them slowly, patiently."
"The olive felt the ancient stone crush her flesh, releasing her soul drop by drop. She flowed into woven palm mats, stacked under a stone press that groaned with age and weight."
"Then came her birth: a golden drop of oil, pure as dawn. It trickled down carved stone channels, joining other drops into a clay jar, and finally into a small glass bottle."
"Inside the bottle, the oil looked at the light and knew she carried the memory of the hills, the touch of old stones, and the taste of centuries.

She was no longer just an olive."
She was Palestine in a drop.
